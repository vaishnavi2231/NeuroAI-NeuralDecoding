## ðŸ”¹ Peripheral Nervous System (PNS)  
 
The PNS connects the CNS to limbs and organs. It is subdivided into:  

- **Somatic Nervous System:**  
  Controls voluntary movement of skeletal muscles, skin, and sensory organs.  

- **Autonomic Nervous System:**  
  Regulates involuntary functions such as heart rate, breathing, and digestion.  
  It manages visceral activities through specialized neurons.

- **Spinal Cord:**  
  Acts as the main communication pathway, conveying **motor control signals** from the brain to muscles and **sensory feedback** from muscles back to the brain.

The brain can be broadly viewed in **three hierarchical layers**, each supporting distinct functions.

### ðŸ§¬ Upper Layer  
**Component:** Neocortex  

The **neocortex** sits at the top of the brain and is composed of approximately:  
- **30 billion neurons**,  
- Each forming around **10,000 synapses**,  
- Arranged in **six layers**, making roughly **300 trillion connections**.  

Each cortical region is specialized for a particular function:  

| Cortical Area | Primary Function |
|----------------|------------------|
| **Occipital Lobe** | Visual processing |
| **Parietal Lobe** | Spatial reasoning & motion processing |
| **Temporal Lobe** | Visual and auditory recognition |
| **Frontal Lobe** | Planning, decision-making & higher cognition |

Additional key structures include:  

- **Basal Ganglia:** Motor control and action selection  
- **Amygdala:** Emotional regulation  
- **Hippocampus:** Memory formation and learning  

The cortex operates as a **hierarchically organized network** of sensory and motor regions â€”  
inputs arrive mainly in **middle cortical layers**, while outputs are generated from **upper and lower layers**.
